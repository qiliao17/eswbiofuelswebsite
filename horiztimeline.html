  1. ESW Biofuels founded

    September 16th, 2013, Cornell University

    Our project team was founded as part of the Engineers for a Sustainable World chapter in order to provide support for the Ithaca FOG bank movement and continues to address biofuels issues related to Cornell’s campus.

  2. ESW Biofuels Team Wins Design Competition

    April 19, 2015, Rochester Institute of Technology

    Cornell Engineering’s Engineers for a Sustainable World (ESW) Biofuels Team recently took first place in a design competition held as part of the 2015 ESW National Conference in Rochester, NY. The Inter-Chapter Design Challenge required student teams to present a plan for integrating already-established technologies to improve the social, technological, and ecological quality of life in Rochester.

  3. Biofuels sits down with Cornell Sun Science as part of their podcast!

    November 4, 2015

    “Ali and Addison sat down with the president of Engineers for a Sustainable World, Mark Henry ’16. Henry, a mechanical engineering student, explained that the club consists of the overarching organization, Engineers for a Sustainable World, as well as four project teams. Each of these four teams — Biofuels, Solar Kiosk, Solar Solutions and Renewable Design (SSRD), and Human Powered Electricity Generation (HPEG) — consists of driven students dedicated to their goal of sustainability.”

  4. ESW Biofuels presents at the Cornell Environmental Collaborative (ECO)

    November 22nd, 2015

    This past weekend, ESW members Max and Addison spoke about the importance of waste fats, oil, and grease collection at ECO’s Waste Weekend event. They enjoyed sharing information about our team’s project with delegates from many other environmental and sustainable organizations throughout campus and also enjoyed the opportunity to learn about other waste prevention initiatives on campus.
